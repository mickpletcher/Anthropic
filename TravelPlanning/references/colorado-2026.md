# Colorado 2026 — 14er Summit Trip

Reference file for the Colorado 14er summits + Theodore Roosevelt National Park trip.

---

## Objectives

1. **Longs Peak** — 14,259 ft, Rocky Mountain National Park
2. **Grays Peak + Torreys Peak** — 14,278 ft / 14,267 ft, double summit in one day
3. **Mount Bierstadt** — 14,060 ft, near Georgetown
4. **Theodore Roosevelt National Park** — North Dakota, en route or as standalone add-on
5. **Half Dome** — Yosemite (lottery submitted for multiple dates — check status)

## Summit Profiles

### Longs Peak
- **Difficulty**: Class 3 (Keyhole Route) — most technical of the group
- **Trailhead**: Longs Peak Trailhead, RMNP (~9,400 ft)
- **Round trip**: 14–16 miles, 5,100 ft gain
- **Typical time**: 10–15 hours car to car
- **Start time**: 3:00–4:00 AM start mandatory (afternoon lightning is lethal above treeline)
- **Permit**: Required for overnight; day hike requires timed entry reservation for RMNP ($2 + park fee)
- **Season**: July–September; snow lingers on the Keyhole and Narrows into July
- **Key hazard**: The Narrows and Homestretch — exposed Class 3 with significant fall risk if wet
- **Gear**: Helmet recommended, trekking poles, layers, headlamp, microspikes if early season

### Grays Peak + Torreys Peak (Double)
- **Difficulty**: Class 1 (Grays), Class 2 (Torreys via standard) — easiest major Colorado double
- **Trailhead**: Grays Peak Trailhead (~11,270 ft) off I-70 near Dillon
- **Round trip**: ~8 miles, 3,000 ft gain for both peaks
- **Typical time**: 5–7 hours
- **Start time**: 5:00–6:00 AM to summit both before noon
- **Permit**: None required; no timed entry
- **Notes**: Extremely accessible from I-70; can do this on the drive in from Denver
- **Gear**: Standard alpine day pack; microspikes if early season

### Mount Bierstadt
- **Difficulty**: Class 2
- **Trailhead**: Guanella Pass Trailhead (~11,669 ft) near Georgetown
- **Round trip**: ~7 miles, 2,800 ft gain
- **Typical time**: 4–6 hours
- **Start time**: 5:00–6:00 AM
- **Permit**: None required; parking reservation at Guanella Pass may be needed on weekends
- **Notes**: Most accessible 14er from Denver (1.5 hrs); good warm-up or cap-off summit
- **Optional extension**: Sawtooth ridge to Mount Evans (Class 3+ scramble)
- **Gear**: Standard day pack

## Acclimatization Strategy

Mick is flying in from Nashville (~600 ft elevation). Altitude sickness risk is real above 8,000 ft if ascending too fast.

**Recommended sequence:**
- Day 1: Arrive Denver (5,280 ft). Sleep in Denver or base town.
- Day 2: Easy hike at moderate elevation (8,000–10,000 ft). No summit attempts.
- Day 3: Grays + Torreys (accessible, trailhead already at 11,270 ft — body gets exposed to 14k without technical risk)
- Day 4: Rest or light activity
- Day 5: Bierstadt
- Day 6: Rest
- Day 7–8: Longs Peak (save for last — most technical, needs full acclimatization)

## Theodore Roosevelt National Park

- **Location**: Medora, North Dakota — ~1,100 miles from Nashville; ~650 miles from Denver
- **Best fit**: Road trip add-on driving BNA → Denver, or Denver → BNA
- **Drive from Denver**: ~9–10 hours
- **Highlights**: Bison herds, painted badlands, prairie dog towns, minimal crowds
- **Best activities**: Petrified Forest Loop hike (10.4 miles), Caprock Coulee Trail, scenic drive
- **Camping**: Cottonwood Campground (RV/tent) — reserve ahead in summer
- **Town**: Medora — small, cowboy-themed, has decent food and lodging
- **Note**: This is genuinely off the beaten path — excellent Facebook content, very "Mick" destination

## Half Dome (Yosemite)

- **Status**: Lottery applications submitted for multiple dates
- **Check**: Yosemite recreation.gov for permit status
- **If permitted**: This becomes its own trip or a California add-on
- **Difficulty**: Class 1 trail + cable section (Class 2 with cables up) — 14–16 miles round trip
- **Key constraint**: Cables are only up mid-May to mid-October (weather dependent)

## Gear Checklist for 14ers

- Trekking poles (collapsible)
- Microspikes (early season — July may still have snow on Longs)
- Helmet (Longs Peak mandatory recommendation)
- Headlamp + extra batteries
- Layers: base layer, insulating mid layer, hard shell
- Gloves and hat (summit temps can be 20–30°F colder than trailhead)
- 3L+ water capacity (hydration pack preferred)
- High-calorie snacks
- Blister kit and moleskin
- Downloaded offline maps (Gaia GPS recommended)
