# Europe Summer 2026 — Spain / Morocco / Portugal

Reference file for the Tour de France + Running of the Bulls trip with Morocco extension.

---

## Trip Overview

**Approximate dates**: Late June / Early July → ~July 18–20
**Budget target**: $2,100–$2,250 total
**Home airport**: BNA (Nashville)

---

## Anchor Events

### Tour de France Grand Départ — Barcelona
- **Dates**: July 4 (ceremonial start / team presentation), July 5 (Stage 1), July 6 (Stage 2)
- **Arrive**: July 3 at latest
- Stage 1 and Stage 2 both start in/around Barcelona
- Standing on roadside is free — positioning matters more than tickets
- Team presentation is ticketed but often has free viewing areas nearby
- Crowds are large but manageable compared to mountain stages

### Running of the Bulls (Encierro) — San Fermín Festival
- **Festival dates**: July 7–14 (runs every morning at 8:00 AM)
- **Bull runs**: Each morning July 7–14, 825 meters through old city streets
- **Participation**: Free to run — register online at the Pamplona city website before July 6
- **Spectator spots**: Fenced barriers along the route, free but arrive by 6 AM; balcony rentals available (~$50–$150)
- **Accommodation warning**: Books out 6–12 months in advance. Prioritize booking immediately.
- Budget accommodations during San Fermín run 3–5x normal rates
- The city is one giant party — sleep is secondary

## Destination Notes

### Barcelona
- **When**: ~July 3–6
- Strong USD value compared to northern Europe; still expensive by global standards
- Neighborhood base: El Born, Gràcia, or Eixample (avoid Las Ramblas hotels)
- Food: Mercado de Santa Caterina over La Boqueria (tourists), pintxos bars in El Born
- Transport: Metro is excellent, no need for taxis within the city
- Day trip option: Montserrat (mountain monastery) if arriving July 2–3 with time to spare

### Pamplona
- **When**: July 7–14
- Small city that explodes to 1M+ during San Fermín
- Old City (Casco Antiguo) is walkable; that's where everything happens
- Accommodation: Book private rooms in guesthouses or apartments — hotels will be gone
- Gear: White clothes + red scarf for the run (traditional); closed-toe shoes mandatory if running
- Evening events: Fireworks, concerts, bull fights (optional), street parties every night

### Tarifa → Morocco Ferry
- **Route**: Tarifa (Spain) → Tangier Med or Tangier Ville
- **Operators**: FRS, Baleària — 35–45 minute crossing
- **Cost**: ~$40–60 round trip
- **Frequency**: Multiple crossings daily
- **Logistics**: Arrive 30–45 min early; passport control on both sides; no visa required for US passport (90 days)
- Tarifa is the southernmost point of Europe — worth a half day itself (kite surfing capital, great seafood)

### Tangier
- **When**: 1–2 nights recommended
- Gateway city — more cosmopolitan than interior Morocco
- Medina is smaller and less overwhelming than Marrakech — good first Morocco experience
- Grand Socco and Petit Socco: main squares, easy to navigate
- Food: Fresh seafood, mint tea, Moroccan pastilla
- **Currency**: Moroccan Dirham (MAD) — exchange at the port or airport; ATMs available
- **USD value**: Very strong. Meals $3–8, private rooms $20–40/night
- **Dress**: Conservative in medina; shorts are fine in tourist areas but blend better in longer pants

### Chefchaouen
- **When**: 2–3 nights
- 3.5 hours from Tangier by CTM bus (~$8) or shared taxi
- The famous blue city — highly photogenic, excellent Facebook/Instagram content
- More relaxed than Tangier; cooler temperatures (higher elevation)
- Good hiking in the Rif Mountains from the city
- Accommodation: Riads in the medina, ~$25–45/night for private room with breakfast
- Book ahead — popular with international travelers

### Madrid
- **When**: 1–2 nights on return
- Direct flights to US from MAD (Madrid Barajas) — check BNA connections
- Alternatively: fly BNA → MAD outbound, return BNA → MAD if Portugal is added
- Retiro Park run in the morning is a solid endurance anchor
- Food: Mercado de San Miguel, Malasaña neighborhood for tapas

### Portugal Extension (Front-End Option)
- **If added**: Fly BNA → LIS (Lisbon), then train/bus to Barcelona for TdF
- Lisbon + Porto combo: 4–5 days
- Lisbon to Barcelona: overnight train (Trenhotel) or budget flight (~$60–100)
- Portugal is one of the best USD-value countries in Western Europe
- Strong cafe culture, excellent seafood, hilly terrain (good for running/cycling)
- Would add ~$400–600 to budget depending on days added

## Booking Priority Order

1. **Pamplona accommodation** — Book immediately; July 7–14 is the hardest lodging in Europe
2. **Flights** — BNA → BCN and MAD → BNA, watch for sales
3. **Chefchaouen riad** — Books up 2–3 months out in summer
4. **Bull run registration** — Opens closer to the event on Pamplona's official site
5. Everything else — Book 4–6 weeks out
